# Decision 0007: Pilot1 Pacing Tracks

## Status

Accepted on June 18, 2026.

## Decision

Pilot1 will support exactly two learner pacing tracks:

1. **Self-paced track**
   Learners use the materials, practice, assessment, remediation, and exam-prep
   resources on their own schedule.
2. **7-day accelerated track**
   Learners follow a compressed seven-day plan.

The 7-day accelerated track must explicitly state these assumptions at the
curriculum level:

- the learner has relevant prerequisite knowledge before starting;
- the learner can commit at least six hours of active learning per day for
  seven days;
- the learner has enough technical maturity and independence to work through
  dense professional-level material;
- the learner can tolerate a high-intensity schedule with limited time for
  remediation;
- mastery gates and exam-readiness standards are not lowered.

## Rationale

Learners do not share one timeline. Some learners need flexible access to
materials over weeks or months. Others may want a compressed exam-preparation
plan. Supporting every possible duration would create unnecessary complexity,
so Pilot1 will support two deliberate routes.

## Consequences

- Pilot1 curriculum materials must identify whether guidance applies to the
  self-paced route, the 7-day accelerated route, or both.
- The 7-day route must be labeled as high-intensity and suitability-bound, not
  as the default recommendation.
- The self-paced route remains the default.
- Both routes preserve the same mastery and exam-readiness gates.
- Future pilots may choose different pacing tracks, but must state them
  explicitly.
